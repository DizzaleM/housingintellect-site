The Problem

Compliance failure is not an option.

Affordable housing compliance teams manage federal mandates with disconnected systems, manual spreadsheets, and tools never designed for this work. The consequences are real.

Fragmented file management

Certifications, findings, documents, and communications scattered across email threads and shared drives — no single source of truth, no audit trail, no visibility.

Manual calculation errors

Income, asset, and student eligibility calculations done by hand in spreadsheets. One error triggers a finding. A pattern triggers recapture.

HOTMA regulatory complexity

The 2025 and 2026 implementation phases are the most significant regulatory overhaul in decades. Most teams are absorbing it without updated systems.

No real-time portfolio visibility

Compliance directors cannot see portfolio-wide risk until audit. There is no early warning system. Problems surface too late to correct.

HOTMA 2025 & 2026

HousingIntellect was built with HOTMA compliance integrated throughout — updated calculation methodologies, revised documentation requirements, and current program rules that reflect what regulators will look for when they audit.

Can a full-time student qualify for a LIHTC unit if their parents don’t claim them as a dependent?
A
Yes — under the independent student exception, a student whose parents do not claim them as a dependent for federal income tax purposes may qualify. Verify with a copy of the parents’ most recent tax return or a signed statement of non-dependency.
IRC §42(i)(3)(D) · HUD 4350.3 Ch. 3
You
What documentation is required in the file?
A
(1) Verification of full-time student status from the institution, (2) prior year tax return from parents or signed non-dependency statement, and (3) a student status certification signed by the household member.

Privacy Policy

Effective April 9, 2026 · Altus Compliance LLC, operator of HousingIntellect

1. Who We Are

HousingIntellect is operated by Altus Compliance LLC, a Georgia limited liability company headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia. This policy explains how we collect, use, protect, and handle information when you use the Platform.

2. Information We Collect

  • Account information: Name, email, organization, job title, and role.
  • Compliance data: Resident information, income and asset documentation, certification records, and related materials.
  • Usage data: Pages visited, features used, actions taken, and timestamps.
  • Security data: Authentication records, session information, failed login attempts, and security event logs.

3. How We Use Your Information

To provide and operate the Platform, authenticate identity, send security alerts, maintain required audit trails, improve the Platform, comply with legal obligations, and enforce our Terms of Service.

4. Resident Data

You are the data controller for resident PII. We process resident data solely to provide Platform services. Resident PII is never transmitted to external AI services. Aria processes compliance query content only.

5. Data Security

We implement field-level encryption for sensitive PII, organizational data isolation at the database layer, HTTPS for all data in transit, 30-minute session timeout, multi-factor authentication for administrative accounts, rate limiting, and immutable audit logging.

6. Data Retention

  • Compliance files and records: 7 years minimum
  • Resident PII: 7 years
  • Audit logs: 7 years
  • Security logs: 1 year
  • Account data: Subscription term plus 30 days

7. We Do Not Sell Your Data

We do not sell your data or your residents’ data to any third party. We do not use data for advertising. HousingIntellect products are ad-free.

8. Data Sharing

Data is shared only with service providers necessary to operate the Platform, all contractually bound to protect it. We may disclose data if required by law or court order.

9. Cookies

We use only session cookies required for authentication and security. No advertising cookies, tracking pixels, or third-party analytics that monitor users across other websites.

10. Your Rights

You may access, correct, delete (subject to retention obligations), and export your data. Contact privacy@housingintellect.io.

11. California Privacy Rights

California residents may have additional CCPA rights. Contact privacy@housingintellect.io.

12. Changes

Material changes communicated by email no fewer than 30 days prior to the effective date.
